## Types of Sports Bets Covered

  

Juiced Bets VIP covers a comprehensive range of betting markets. Understanding these different bet types is crucial for making the most of their picks.

  

### Moneyline Bets

  

Moneyline bets are the simplest form of sports wagering. You're simply picking which team will win the game outright, with no point spreads involved.

  

The favored team pays lower odds than the underdog. This creates an enticement to bet on underdogs for better payouts.

  

Juiced Bets VIP often includes moneyline picks when they identify clear value on either favorites or underdogs.

  

### Point Spread Bets

  

Point spread betting is the most common type of wager in American sports betting. The bookmaker assigns a number that handicaps one team and favors another.

  

For example, if the Miami Heat are favored by 3.5 points over the Oklahoma City Thunder, Miami must win by 4 or more points for spread bets on them to cash.

  

The half-point eliminates the possibility of a push (tie). Juiced Bets VIP frequently targets spread bets when they see line movement opportunities.

  

### Over/Under (Total) Bets

  

Over/Under bets focus on the combined scoring of both teams. You're wagering whether the total points will be over or under the bookmaker's set number.

  

If an NBA game has a total of 215.5 points, over bettors need 216+ combined points to win. Under bettors need 215 or fewer points.

  

These bets are popular because you don't need to pick a winner - just whether it'll be a high or low-scoring game.

  

### Parlay Bets

  

Parlays combine multiple bets into one wager for a larger potential payout. All selections must win for the parlay to cash.

  

A two-team parlay might combine a Lakers moneyline bet with a Warriors spread bet. Both must hit for you to win.

  

Parlays are appealing because they pay much more than individual bets. However, they're significantly harder to win consistently.

  

**Important Note**: Parlays account for over 70% of sportsbook revenue each month in the US. This tells you they favor the house long-term.

  

### Teaser Bets

  

Teasers are modified parlays that give bettors better odds at reduced payouts. You can move point spreads in your favor across multiple games.

  

For example, a 6-point NBA teaser might change a -3 favorite to +3, and a +7 underdog to +13. Both adjusted bets must still win.

  

While you get better odds of winning, the payout is reduced compared to a standard parlay.

  

### Proposition Bets

  

Proposition bets (props) focus on specific outcomes within a game that aren't related to the final score. These can include:

  

-   Player statistics (points, rebounds, assists)
-   Game events (first touchdown scorer)
-   Quarter/half results
-   Team-specific achievements

  

Props have become increasingly popular, especially during major events like the Super Bowl.

  

### Futures Bets

  

Futures are long-term wagers on events that won't be decided for weeks or months. Common examples include:

  

-   Season championship winners
-   Individual awards (MVP, Rookie of the Year)
-   Season win totals for teams

  

The best payouts come from betting early in the season. As outcomes become more predictable, odds decrease accordingly.

  

### Live Betting (In-Play)

  

Live betting allows you to place wagers while games are in progress. This has revolutionized sports betting by creating continuous betting opportunities.

  

You can bet on constantly changing lines, momentum shifts, and real-time developments. Popular live betting markets include:

  

-   Adjusted point spreads
-   Live totals
-   Next team to score
-   Quarter/period results

  

**Caution**: Live betting can be risky for problem gamblers due to its fast-paced, continuous nature.

  

### Round Robin Bets

  

Round robin bets create multiple smaller parlays from a larger group of selections. If you pick 4 teams, you might create six 2-team parlays instead of one 4-team parlay.

  

This approach reduces risk while maintaining higher payout potential than straight bets.

  

### Reverse Forecast Bets

  

More common in racing and individual sports, reverse forecast bets involve picking the top two finishers in either order.

  

### Middle Bets

  

Middle betting involves placing wagers on both sides of a game at different lines, hoping the final result falls between both numbers for a double win.

## Legal Considerations and Responsible Gambling

  

Before joining any sports betting service, it's crucial to understand the legal landscape and practice responsible gambling.

  

### Sports Betting Legality in the United States

  

The legal status of sports betting has changed dramatically in recent years. The Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act of 1992 (PASPA) previously made sports betting illegal in most states.

  

Delaware, Montana, Nevada, and Oregon were grandfathered in with existing sports betting frameworks. PASPA was struck down by the Supreme Court in Murphy v. National Collegiate Athletic Association in 2018.

  

This landmark decision paved the way for individual states to legalize sports betting. Since then, the industry has exploded.

  

### Current Legal Status

  

As of January 2024:

-   **37 states, Washington D.C., and Puerto Rico** have operational legalized sports betting
-   **North Carolina** has legalized sports betting with operations beginning later in 2024
-   **30 states, Washington D.C., and Puerto Rico** have legalized online sports betting
-   **8 states** only allow betting through physical sportsbooks

  

Over $20 billion was spent on sports betting in the United States by May 2020, just two years after PASPA was struck down.

  

**Important**: Always verify that sports betting is legal in your specific jurisdiction before participating. Laws vary by state, city, and local municipality.

  

### Responsible Gambling Practices

  

Sports betting should be treated as entertainment, not as a way to make money or solve financial problems.

  

**Key Guidelines:**

-   **Set strict budget limits** - Never bet more than you can afford to lose
-   **Track your spending** - Know exactly how much you're wagering
-   **Take regular breaks** - Don't bet every single day
-   **Avoid chasing losses** - Never increase bet sizes to recover losses quickly
-   **Seek help if needed** - Problem gambling resources are available

  

**Warning Signs:**

-   Betting more than planned
-   Hiding betting activity from family/friends
-   Borrowing money to bet
-   Feeling anxious or depressed about betting results
-   Neglecting responsibilities due to betting
